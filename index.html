Real-world systems are dynamic with system state changing over time, either discretly or continuously. Decisions are made at specific times to bring the system to an optimal state ("optimal" defined differently e.g. minimal cost, system stability or other customer-centric metrices).

These decisions are based on available data, current state, system constraints and expected future state. This work provides a simple taxonomy of attributes of different real-time decision making models.

A small note:
The taxonomy is not strict and many problems can be placed under different classification and may not have all the attributes of that particular classification. Lyapunov Optimization is the focus for examples.
